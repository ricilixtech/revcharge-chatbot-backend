⚡ CHARGER TYPES & FEATURES

25. What is the difference between tethered and untethered chargers?

Tethered chargers have a permanently attached cable.

Untethered chargers use a detachable cable, giving a cleaner appearance.

26. What is the difference between smart and basic chargers?

Smart chargers include:

App control

Charging schedules

Energy monitoring

Solar integration

Load balancing

Basic chargers provide simple plug-in charging without smart features.

27. What is single-phase charging?

Single-phase charging is standard in most Australian homes and adds approximately 30–40 km of driving range per hour.

28. What is three-phase charging?

Three-phase charging is faster and can add up to 120 km of range per hour.
It requires a three-phase electrical connection.

29. What is a Level 2 charger?

Level 2 chargers are 5–7 times faster than a standard household outlet and typically charge a vehicle fully in 4–8 hours.

30. Which charger should I choose?

The best charger depends on your:

Vehicle

Electrical connection

Solar system

Charging needs

Revcharge checks charger specifications in the inventory database and provides a suitable recommendation.

Grid electricity

34. What is bidirectional charging?

Bidirectional charging allows electricity to flow both ways.

V2H (Vehicle-to-Home) – your EV can power your home.
V2G (Vehicle-to-Grid) – your EV can send electricity back to the grid.

🔌 CHARGING & USAGE

35. How fast is home charging?

A standard outlet may take 24+ hours.

A 7kW Level 2 charger typically charges a vehicle in 4–8 hours.

A 22kW three-phase charger may charge in about 1–2 hours.

36. How much does it cost to charge an EV at home?

A full charge typically costs $10–$20 depending on electricity rates.

37. How often do EV owners charge?

Most EV owners charge 2–4 times per week, often overnight.

38. Is overnight charging safe?

Yes. Chargers include safety features such as:

Temperature monitoring

Automatic shut-off

39. What happens during a power outage?

Charging stops safely and automatically resumes when power returns.

40. Will charging overload my home?

Smart chargers include load management to balance electricity usage and prevent overload.
